Think Bee

‘Think Bee’ is an industrial microarchitecture brick that aims to provide key habitat to both burrowing and nesting bee species within urban residential environments. The project responds to a custom ‘reinventing the brick’ brief. I undertook this project as a part of my ANU capstone individual project. The project provided a system for randomised organic microterrain generation, within the space of the brick. The goal being to bring natural forms and habitats back into residential architectures. The projects final outcomes included, standard product renders, upscaled sculptural renders, and a physical material test, consisting of a fired cardboard clay material.


Logan Student Center

This was some work that I did for my capstone group project at the ANU. The project had us working with a client to produce a complete interior design redesign of the Griffith University Logan Student Centre. Our solution had to be catered to the theme of community and our group heavily focused on accessibility in the space.

Air Aware

The Air Aware system responded to a project brief requiring us to produce a socially active product able to engage with its environment through utilization of both inputs and outputs. We took on this brief utilising the Arduino physical computing system in combination with 3D printed parts. We decided to tackle the issue of decreasing air quality. Our system aimed to discretely monitor air quality data, and provide a website communication network through which this data could be communicated wirelessly. The physical prototype is designed to discretely integrate under the bottom of a table so that it can be hidden from landlords. It monitors 5 different air quality inputs. See mockup for handheld alternative in the bottom right.


Botanical Blocks

This product responded to a student led group brief which had us produce a product to tackle a wicked problem. We landed on the issue of a ‘Lack of Environmental Accessibility’ in urban rental spaces.
After an extensive interviewing and research process we found that renters lack the ability to own, maintain and keep green spaces in their homes. The Botanical Block system attacks this problem by providing a tile-able, stackable vertical gardening system geared for indoor spaces. The system is designed to efficiently integrate into indoors spaces, is highly portable, and low maintenance.


Mycelium Chair

This chair responded to a design brief which asked students to ‘reveal a system of the unseen’. In order to respond to this brief I established a hypothetical client which would be the ‘Botanical Gardens Canberra’. The client goal was to reveal unseen elements of the forest environment for an exhibition event. My process targeted unseen mycelium structures that exist within the forest floor.
The work consisted of two parts: The legs, which represented the mycelium, and the seat being the mushroom cap itself. This was created by taking and processing real mycelium data, and transferring this into fusion to create 3d assets. The seat consisted of three leather pillow structures, constructed for comfort.

Precise Drawing and Model Making

During my second year of uni I took a class which gave me industry standard precise modelling, isometric and perspective drawing skills. I have taken these skills with me, and they continue to play a central part in my design practise.
Works on the right responded to a brief which wanted us to create a socially active, tile-able product prototype . We were to present this using a series of models and 2 point perspective drawings.
I ended up producing a design for a skatepark, aiming at creating positive recreational facilities for kids in urban communities. As curves cannot be accurately produced using perspective drawings, we were told to map them with precise segments.
Habitecture

This group project comes from a brief which had us producing a working scale habitat for an endangered species of local Canberra birds. Our species was the ‘gang gang’ cockatoo, an iconic local Canberra bird that is losing breeding habitat due to urbanisation and sprawl.
Our goal was to reintegrate this bird back into the urban environments of canberra cities by creating a nesting environment built to be attached to houses and buildings. The design we landed was made to integrate with the aesthetics of local canberra architecture. The product was constructed from strong insulating decking wood, and featured a soft interior perfect for nesting.

GangGang Redesign

In this project our brief had us designing an entirely new brand image for the local Downer cafe ‘Gang Gang’.
My take on the brief was that I felt the cafe need to integrate the younger crowd that visited the cafe at night for live music gigs, with the older audience who visited commonly during the day. I ended up doing this by creating a lighthearted blend which mixed 60-70’s style cartoon print techniques and photography commically with iconic Aussie punk rock scene aesthetics and imagery, creating a fun parodied combination.
